Hello, I’m Aleah! I am currently an aspiring Content Designer, and hope to pursue my journey through my passion for creative writing.

My Journey:

I’ve always been an inherent creator, starting from a young age. During my years in elementary, middle school, and now high school, I’ve found that my spurs of creation are best shown through written words on a page. I quickly began to develop this skill through my required Pre-AP English classes at my high school, registering for a public speaking course at Austin Community College in summer of 2021, SPCH- 1315-008, and as of recently, entering into a national contest known as the Eon Essay Contest on The Precipice, in summer of 2022.

As a novice in the field of Content Design, I hope to mature in various styles of writing, thoroughly conveying my ideas, and reaching my target audience. My goal is to hone these skills as best as I can, in order for me to one day become an experienced Content Designer. I’m grateful for any opportunity to improve and grow, and am determined to expand my writing as soon as I possibly can.

My Hobbies and Interests:

I’ve always enjoyed reading. When I was younger, I loved books filled with short fables; they allowed me to enter otherwise unattainable realities. Now, I indulge myself in the different worlds that lie from cover to cover, as I temporarily assume the roles of the protagonists. I’ve learned over time that it was not just the academics of reading that I enjoyed, but the ability to reach foreign realms through literature.

Journaling has been a significant pillar in my life for as long as I can remember. It was never something I kept up with daily, but at times when a younger me would become too consumed in my emotions, I felt I could get them out best when I wrote about them. Through this, my pencil not only became my voice for me when I couldn’t use my own, but also allowed me to learn my own strengths and weaknesses when it came to writing.

My love for Anime dates back to my years as a young child as well. I enjoy the moments spent with my dad, and now my younger sister too, in tackling the abstract ideals of eastern philosophy while taking an unforgettable adventure.
